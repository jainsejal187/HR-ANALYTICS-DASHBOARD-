Research & Development (R&D) has the highest turnover, followed by Sales.

Lower salaries, especially in Sales and early-tenure R&D roles, are associated with higher attrition.
Work-life balance improves with tenure, indicating that stability increases satisfaction.
Yes, employees with promotion gaps over 10 years show declining job satisfaction and higher likelihood of leaving.
Mid-career employees (7–12 years experience) in R&D and Sales have higher attrition, while very senior employees are more stable.
Key factors include department (R&D, Sales), job satisfaction, promotion gaps, tenure, and education background. Addressing these can improve retention.
